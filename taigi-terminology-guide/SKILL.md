# Taigi Terminology Guide

This skill ensures that all work related to the Taigi language follows the preferred linguistic terminology.

## Core Rules

1.  **Language Name:** Always refer to the language as **Taigi**.
    *   **Avoid:** "Taiwanese Southern Min", "Holo", "Taiwanese Hokkien", or "Taibun" (unless specifically referring to Han-Lo).
2.  **Terminology:** Use the term **Roman Orthography** (or "roman orthography") instead of "Romanization" or "romanization".
3.  **Orthography Names:**
    *   Use **Taigi Latin-script Orthography** or **Taigi Roman-script Orthography**.
    *   Use **KPL (Kàu-io̍k Pō͘ Lô-má-jī)** for the official standard.
    *   **Note:** Historically, "Tâi-lô" was used to refer to POJ (Peh-ōe-jī), but for the modern official standard, use **KPL**.
    *   **Avoid:** "Taiwanese Romanization", "TL", or using "Tâi-lô" for the modern standard.

## Why This Matters

Standardizing terminology respects the linguistic identity of Taigi and aligns with modern academic and cultural preferences in Taiwan.
